Hi, I'm Ryan.

I built Terrapin for the people I grew up watching work.

The shoebox of receipts

My mom is a Filipino immigrant. She ran a catering business for twenty years, almost entirely on paper. At tax time she'd sit at the kitchen table with a shoebox of receipts, a calculator, and a legal pad. It would take her days.

There were tools out there. None of them were built for her. They assumed you had an IT department, or at minimum a comfort with software that she didn't have time to learn. They were built for "businesses" in the abstract — not for the woman who'd been up since 4am baking lumpia for a wedding she was catering that night.

She didn't need enterprise software. She needed a tool that opened, did one thing well, and got out of her way.

The taco stand

Years later I met a Spanish-speaking taco vendor working out of a metal cart near a busy corner in LA. He had no POS system. No card reader. He handled cash out of a locked metal box and kept track of orders with a paper notepad. His business ran on memory and hustle.

I asked if he'd want a simple order-logging app. He said yes, but only if it worked without an account, without the internet, and without charging him monthly — because his cousin had tried an app once and it drained his phone plan and then charged him $9.99 a month for something he never used.

That moment is when Terrapin stopped being a side project and became a mission.

The mission, in plain terms

Terrapin is a library of small tools for the people who built America. Immigrant-owned businesses. Contractors. Caterers. Food truck owners. Freelancers. The people whose businesses run out of a truck, a shop, or a kitchen table.

Every tool works in English and Spanish. Every tool runs on your computer. Your data never leaves your machine. There's nothing to leak. Nothing to breach. Nothing to sell.

Privacy isn't a feature. It's the architecture.

How I make money

Every core tool is free. Always. No login, no subscription, no trial.

Premium tools are one-time purchases. You buy once and own it forever. Smart Receipt Box is the first — $29, snap a photo of any receipt, local AI extracts every detail in ten seconds. I make money from the tool, not from your data. That alignment matters.

I chose the harder business model on purpose.

The human part

I'm one person building this. No team, no VC, no data-science consultancy pretending to be AI. I answer every support email myself. I read every piece of feedback.

If Terrapin helps you, I want to know. If it breaks, I want to know more. If you have an idea for the next tool, tell me.

The Grateful Dead thing

Terrapin Station is a Grateful Dead album. The name is a nod to the origin of this project — which grew out of a conversation about building a personal wiki. The Dead built a community around free tape trading. Terrapin builds a library around free tools. Distribution is the product.

The lightning bolt on the turtle's shell is for the people who get it. Everyone else just sees a reliable turtle with a tool belt.
